Description

Chinese Fried Noodles is a delicious and flavorful stir-fry dish made with thin noodles, crisp vegetables, and a savory sauce.

The combination of stir-fried broccoli, carrots, bell peppers, and cabbage adds color, texture, and nutrients,

while the noodles soak up the rich umami flavors of soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil.

Ingredients

Dough

  • 200g egg noodles (or any thin wheat noodles)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Filling

  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1/2 cup bean sprouts (optional)
  • 200g chicken, beef, shrimp, or tofu (optional)

For the Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (or hoisin sauce for vegetarian)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

Steps to make Chinese Fried Noodles

  1. Prepare the Noodles

    1. Cook the noodles according to the package instructions until just al dente.
    2. Drain and rinse with cold water to prevent sticking.
    3. Toss with 1 tablespoon of oil and set aside.

  2. Prepare the Sauce

    1. In a small bowl, mix soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, sugar, and black pepper.

  3. Stir-Fry the Ingredients

    1. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat.
    2. If using meat or tofu, stir-fry until fully cooked, then set aside.
    3. In the same pan, stir-fry onions and garlic for 30 seconds.
    4. Add carrots, bell peppers, and cabbage, cooking for 2 minutes until slightly softened.
    5. Add broccoli florets and stir-fry for another 2 minutes. If needed, add a splash of water to help them cook.

  4. Fry the Noodles

    1. Add the cooked noodles to the pan and pour in the sauce.
    2. Stir-fry everything together for 2â€“3 minutes, ensuring the noodles absorb the sauce.
    3. Return the meat or tofu (if using) and mix well.
    4. Add bean sprouts last for a slight crunch.

  5. Serve & Enjoy

    1. Garnish with green onions or sesame seeds (optional).
    2. Serve hot with chili sauce or extra soy sauce on the side.
