Pelmeni

Home

Description

Pelmeni are traditional Russian dumplings made of thin dough filled with seasoned meat,

usually a mix of pork, beef, or lamb. They are boiled and served with butter,

sour cream, or vinegar, making them a hearty and flavorful dish.

Ingredients

Dough

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Filling

  • 250g ground pork
  • 250g ground beef (or lamb)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

For Serving

  • Butter
  • Sour cream
  • Ketchup

Steps to make Pelmeni

  1. Prepare the Dough

    1. In a large bowl, mix flour and salt.
    2. Add the egg and slowly pour in water, mixing until a dough forms.
    3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5–7 minutes until smooth.
    4. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes while you prepare the filling.

  2. Make the Filling

    1. In a bowl, mix ground pork, beef (or lamb), chopped onion, garlic, salt, and black pepper.
    2. Stir until well combined. The filling should be moist but not runny.

  3. Assemble the Pelmeni

    1. Roll out the dough thinly (about 2mm thick) on a floured surface.
    2. Cut out small circles using a glass or a round cutter (about 2 inches in diameter).
    3. Place a small teaspoon of filling in the center of each circle.
    4. Fold the dough over the filling into a half-moon shape and pinch the edges tightly.
    5. Bring the two corners together and pinch again to form the classic pelmeni shape.

  4. Cook the Pelmeni

    1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
    2. Drop the pelmeni in and stir gently to prevent sticking.
    3. Boil for 4–5 minutes, or until they float to the top and cook for another minute.
    4. Remove with a slotted spoon and serve immediately.

  5. Serve & Enjoy

    1. Serve hot with butter, sour cream, or ketchup.
    2. Optional: Sprinkle with black pepper, fresh herbs, or a touch of mustard.
Enjoy your homemade pelmeni!
