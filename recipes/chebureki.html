Chebureki

Home

Description

Chebureki are crispy, deep-fried turnovers filled with seasoned ground meat,

traditionally made with beef or lamb. They have a thin, flaky dough that becomes golden and crunchy when fried,

while the filling remains juicy and flavorful. These savory pastries are popular in Eastern European and Central Asian cuisines,

especially in Russia, Ukraine, and the Caucasus region.

Ingredients

Dough

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Filling

  • 250g ground beef or lamb
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin (optional)
  • 2–3 tablespoons water (to keep the filling juicy)

For Frying

  • 1–2 cups vegetable oil (for deep frying)

For Serving

  • Sauces of your choice

Steps to make Chebureki

  1. Prepare the Dough

    1. In a large bowl, mix flour and salt.
    2. Add warm water and vegetable oil, kneading until a smooth dough forms.
    3. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes while you prepare the filling.

  2. Make the Filling

    1. In a bowl, mix ground meat, chopped onion, garlic, salt, black pepper, and cumin.
    2. Add a few tablespoons of water to keep the filling moist.

  3. Assemble the Chebureki

    1. Divide the dough into small balls (about the size of a golf ball).
    2. Roll out each ball into a thin 6-8 inch circle.
    3. Place a thin layer of filling on one half of the circle, leaving a small border.
    4. Fold the dough over the filling and press the edges firmly to seal (you can use a fork for a decorative edge).

  4. Fry the Chebureki

    1. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan over medium-high heat.
    2. Fry each cheburek for 2–3 minutes per side, until golden brown and crispy.
    3. Remove and place on paper towels to drain excess oil.

  5. Serve & Enjoy

    1. Serve hot, optionally with sour cream or your favorite sauce on the side.
    2. Optional: Sprinkle with fresh herbs.
Enjoy your homemade Chebureki!
